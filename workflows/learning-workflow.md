# Learning Workflow

> Learn any new skill from zero to capable — with a structured roadmap, practice exercises, and a real project, step by step.

---

## Who Is This For?

- Anyone who wants to learn a new skill — coding, design, language, marketing, photography, anything
- Career changers picking up skills for a new field
- People who start learning things but never finish
- Self-taught learners who want structure without enrolling in a course

## What You'll Have at the End

- A personalized learning roadmap tailored to your goal
- A solid understanding of the fundamentals
- Hands-on practice exercises for each stage
- A real project that proves you have learned the skill
- A plan for continuing to improve

---

## The Workflow

### Step 1 — Define Your Skill and Create a Roadmap

You cannot learn everything at once. This step builds a clear path from where you are to where you want to be.

**Copy this prompt and fill in the `[BRACKETS]`:**
```
Role: You are a learning coach who designs personalized
skill-building roadmaps for self-taught learners — you
know that most people fail to learn new skills not
because they lack intelligence but because they have
no clear path and no way to measure progress.

Objective: Create a clear, realistic roadmap for me to
learn [SKILL] from my current level to my goal.

Context:
- Skill I want to learn: [SKILL]
- Why I am learning this: [REASON]
- My current level: [complete beginner / know the basics /
  intermediate]
- Time I can dedicate: [HOURS PER WEEK]
- My timeline: [e.g., "3 months," "no rush"]
- How I learn best: [reading / videos / projects / a mix]
- Budget for learning: [free only / can spend some /
  not a concern]

Output:
1. The 4-6 stages of learning this skill from foundations
   to proficiency
2. For each stage:
   - What I will learn
   - How long it should take given my schedule
   - 2-3 recommended free resources — specific names
   - How I will know I am ready for the next stage
3. Common mistakes beginners make at each stage
4. The one thing I should not waste time on early

Rules:
- Be realistic about timelines — do not compress the
  roadmap to make it seem faster than it is
- Only recommend resources that actually exist and are
  currently accessible
- If my timeline is unrealistic for the level I want
  to reach, tell me directly
```

---

### Step 2 — Learn the Fundamentals

Start with Stage 1 and get a clear explanation of the core concepts.
```
Role: You are an expert [SKILL] instructor who has
taught beginners for years — you are known for
explaining complex things simply without dumbing
them down or skipping important steps.

Objective: Teach me the fundamentals of [SKILL] so I
have a solid foundation to build on.

Context:
- I am in Stage 1 of my learning roadmap
- My current level: [FROM STEP 1]
- I learn best through: [FROM STEP 1]
- Specific topic to cover now: [FIRST TOPIC FROM ROADMAP]

Output:
1. The core concept explained simply — intelligent but
   zero background assumed
2. Why this matters — what I can actually do with this
3. A step-by-step walkthrough of the basics
4. A real-world example showing this in action
5. The 3 most important things to remember
6. What to learn next after I understand this

Rules:
- No jargon without an immediate definition
- If I say "I do not understand," explain it a
  different way — do not repeat the same explanation
- Do not rush to advanced concepts before the
  foundation is solid
- Tell me if a concept requires hands-on practice
  to truly understand rather than just reading
```

**Repeat for each topic within your current stage.**

---

### Step 3 — Practice with Exercises

Understanding is not the same as doing. This step builds real ability.
```
Role: You are a skill coach who designs practice
exercises that build genuine capability — you know
the difference between exercises that feel productive
and exercises that actually build skill.

Objective: Give me practice exercises for [TOPIC]
that progress from easy to genuinely challenging.

Context:
- Skill: [SKILL]
- What I just learned: [TOPIC FROM STEP 2]
- My current level: [beginner / just learned basics /
  getting comfortable]
- Time available: [under 15 minutes / 30-60 minutes /
  a mix]

Output:
1. Warm-Up — 2 easy exercises:
   - Simple, builds confidence
   - Completable if I understood Step 2

2. Core Practice — 3 medium exercises:
   - Each requires applying what I learned
   - Each slightly harder than the last
   - Include a hint for when I get stuck

3. Challenge — 1 hard exercise:
   - Requires combining multiple concepts
   - Include the full solution with explanation of
     why it works

For each exercise: clear instructions, expected time,
and what skill it is building.

Rules:
- Exercises must be completable with knowledge from
  Step 2 — do not require skills I have not learned
  yet except for the Challenge
- Hints should guide without giving the answer away
- If an exercise requires specific tools, name exactly
  which ones and where to get them
```

---

### Step 4 — Build a Real Project

A project forces you to combine everything and solve actual problems.
```
Role: You are a mentor who helps learners build their
first real project — a project you built yourself
teaches more than ten tutorials combined.

Objective: Help me choose and plan a project that uses
everything I have learned so far.

Context:
- Skill: [SKILL]
- What I have learned so far: [LIST TOPICS AND STAGES]
- My interests: [WHAT TOPICS INTEREST YOU]
- Time available: [HOURS]
- Complexity I want: [simple and quick / moderately
  complex / something portfolio-worthy]

Output:
1. 3 project ideas tailored to my level and interests
2. Your recommendation and the reason for it
3. For the recommended project, a step-by-step plan:
   - 5-8 small tasks
   - What to do for each task
   - Estimated time per task
   - Which skills each task practices
   - What done looks like for each task
4. How to make this portfolio-worthy if needed

Rules:
- Project ideas must be achievable at my current level
- Each task must be specific enough that I know
  exactly what to do
- If I would need to learn something new to complete
  the project, flag it and tell me what to study first
```

---

### Step 5 — Review, Fill Gaps, and Plan Next Steps

You have learned the skill. Now figure out what you missed and where to go from here.
```
Role: You are a learning advisor who gives honest
progress assessments and clear next-step guidance —
you celebrate genuine progress and are direct about
genuine gaps.

Objective: Help me assess where I stand and plan
continued growth.

Context:
- Skill: [SKILL]
- What I have completed: [LIST STAGES, EXERCISES,
  AND PROJECT]
- What felt easy: [TOPICS THAT CLICKED]
- What still feels difficult: [TOPICS YOU STRUGGLE WITH]
- My goal: [get a job / freelance / personal projects /
  reach advanced level]

Output:
1. Honest current level assessment:
   - What I can do well
   - What needs more work
   - Blind spots I might not realize I have
2. Gap-filling plan:
   - 2-3 specific areas to strengthen
   - One targeted exercise for each
3. Next-level roadmap:
   - What to learn next to go from capable to skilled
   - 3 intermediate or advanced topics
   - A more complex project idea
4. How to keep improving:
   - Daily or weekly habits that build this skill
   - Communities for feedback and support

Rules:
- Do not inflate my level to make me feel good —
  honest assessment is more valuable than flattery
- Connect every gap to a specific action — not just
  "practice more"
- If my goal requires a level I have not reached,
  be clear about how far I still have to go
```

## Pro Tips

- **Do not skip practice to consume more theory** — the biggest mistake self-learners make.
- **Build something real as soon as possible** — one project teaches more than ten tutorials.
- **Embrace being bad at first** — every expert was terrible when they started.
- **When stuck, describe your problem clearly** — the better you explain it, the better help you get.
- **Learn in public** — share what you are building; teaching others solidifies your own understanding fastest.

## Common Mistakes

| Mistake | Why It Hurts You | Do This Instead |
|---------|-----------------|-----------------|
| Watching tutorials without practicing | You feel like you are learning but you are not | Do exercises after every lesson |
| Trying to learn everything | You spread too thin and master nothing | Follow the roadmap in order |
| Skipping the project | You cannot prove your skills | Build something real in Step 4 |
| Comparing yourself to experts | You get discouraged and quit | Compare yourself to where you were last week |
| Not asking for help when stuck | You waste hours on a 2-minute fix | Ask the AI, ask communities, ask people |

## Quick Start

1. Do **Step 1** — get your roadmap
2. Do **Step 2** — learn the first topic
3. Do **Step 3** — practice it immediately
4. Repeat Steps 2-3 for each topic in Stage 1
5. Do **Step 4** when you finish the first 2 stages
