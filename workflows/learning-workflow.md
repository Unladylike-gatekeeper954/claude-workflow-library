# 🎯 Learning Workflow

> Learn any new skill from zero to capable — with a structured roadmap, practice exercises, and a real project, step by step.

---

## Who Is This For?

- Anyone who wants to learn a new skill (coding, design, language, instrument, marketing, photography — anything)
- Career changers picking up skills for a new field
- People who start learning things but never finish
- Self-taught learners who want structure without enrolling in a course

## What You'll Have at the End

- A personalized learning roadmap tailored to your goal
- An understanding of the fundamentals
- Hands-on practice exercises for each stage
- A real project that proves you've learned the skill
- A plan for continuing to improve

---

## The Workflow

### Step 1 — Define Your Skill and Create a Roadmap

You can't learn everything at once. This step builds a clear path from where you are to where you want to be.

**Copy this prompt and fill in the `[BRACKETS]`:**

```
Role: You are a learning coach who designs personalized
skill-building roadmaps for self-taught learners.

Objective: Create a clear, realistic roadmap for me to
learn [SKILL] from my current level to my goal level.

Context:
- Skill I want to learn: [SKILL — e.g., "Python
  programming," "graphic design," "public speaking,"
  "guitar," "copywriting"]
- Why I'm learning this: [REASON — career change, side
  project, personal interest, job requirement]
- My current level: [complete beginner / I know the
  basics / intermediate]
- Time I can dedicate: [HOURS PER WEEK]
- My timeline: [e.g., "3 months," "6 months," "no rush"]
- How I learn best: [reading / watching videos /
  doing projects / a mix of everything]
- Budget for learning: [free only / can spend some money /
  budget doesn't matter]

Output: Create a learning roadmap with:
1. The 4-6 stages of learning this skill (from
   foundations to proficiency)
2. For each stage:
   - What I'll learn
   - How long it should take (given my schedule)
   - 2-3 recommended free resources (specific names,
     not vague suggestions)
   - How I'll know I'm ready to move to the next stage
3. Common mistakes beginners make at each stage
4. The one thing I should NOT waste time on early
```

---

### Step 2 — Learn the Fundamentals

Start with Stage 1 from your roadmap. Get a clear, simple explanation of the core concepts.

```
Role: You are an expert [SKILL] instructor who teaches
beginners using clear language and relatable examples.

Objective: Teach me the fundamentals of [SKILL] so I
have a solid foundation to build on.

Context:
- I'm in Stage 1 of my learning roadmap
- My current level: [FROM STEP 1]
- I learn best through: [FROM STEP 1]
- Specific area to cover now: [FIRST TOPIC FROM YOUR
  ROADMAP]

Output: Teach me this topic with:
1. The core concept explained simply (imagine I'm 15
   years old and smart but have zero background)
2. Why this matters — what can I DO with this knowledge?
3. A step-by-step walkthrough of the basics
4. A real-world example showing this in action
5. The 3 most important things to remember
6. What to learn next after I understand this

No jargon without explanation. If you must use a
technical term, define it immediately.
```

**Repeat this for each topic within your current stage.**

**Helpful follow-ups:**

- `I'm confused about [SPECIFIC PART]. Can you explain it differently?`
- `Can you give me 3 more examples?`
- `What's the difference between [CONCEPT A] and [CONCEPT B]?`

---

### Step 3 — Practice with Exercises

Understanding isn't the same as doing. This step gives you hands-on practice.

```
Role: You are a skill coach who designs practice
exercises that build real ability, not just knowledge.

Objective: Give me practice exercises for [TOPIC/STAGE]
that go from easy to challenging.

Context:
- Skill: [SKILL]
- What I just learned: [TOPIC FROM STEP 2]
- My level right now: [beginner / just learned the basics /
  getting comfortable]
- I want: [quick exercises I can do in 15 minutes /
  deeper exercises that take 30-60 minutes / a mix]

Output: Give me:
1. Warm-Up (2 easy exercises):
   - Simple, builds confidence
   - I should be able to do these if I understood Step 2

2. Core Practice (3 medium exercises):
   - Requires me to apply what I learned
   - Each one slightly harder than the last
   - Include hints I can look at if I'm stuck

3. Challenge (1 hard exercise):
   - Pushes me beyond what I've practiced
   - Might require combining multiple concepts
   - Include the solution with explanation

For each exercise:
- What to do (clear instructions)
- Expected time to complete
- What I'll learn from doing it
```

**After completing exercises, ask:**

```
I completed the exercises. Here's where I struggled:
[DESCRIBE]. Give me 2 more exercises targeting my
weak spots, and explain the concepts I'm missing.
```

---

### Step 4 — Build a Real Project

This is where real learning happens. A project forces you to combine everything and solve real problems.

```
Role: You are a mentor who helps learners build their
first real project — something they can show to others
with pride.

Objective: Help me choose and plan a project that uses
everything I've learned so far.

Context:
- Skill: [SKILL]
- What I've learned so far: [LIST THE TOPICS/STAGES
  YOU'VE COMPLETED]
- My interests: [WHAT TOPICS INTEREST YOU — this makes
  the project more fun and motivating]
- Time I can spend on the project: [HOURS]
- I want the project to be: [simple and quick /
  moderately complex / impressive for a portfolio]

Output:
1. 3 project ideas tailored to my level and interests
   (with a one-line description of each)
2. Your recommendation and why
3. For the recommended project, a step-by-step plan:
   - Break it into 5-8 small tasks
   - What to do for each task
   - Estimated time per task
   - Which skills each task practices
   - What "done" looks like for each task
4. How to make this project portfolio-worthy (if I
   want to show it to employers or clients)
```

**As you build, ask for help:**

```
I'm working on [TASK] of my project and I'm stuck on
[SPECIFIC PROBLEM]. Here's what I've tried: [WHAT YOU
TRIED]. Help me figure this out — but explain WHY the
solution works so I actually learn.
```

---

### Step 5 — Review, Fill Gaps, and Plan Next Steps

You've learned the skill. Now figure out what you missed and where to go from here.

```
Role: You are a learning advisor who helps people
assess their progress and plan continued growth.

Objective: Help me figure out where I stand with
[SKILL] and what to focus on next.

Context:
- Skill: [SKILL]
- What I've completed: [LIST STAGES, EXERCISES, AND
  PROJECT]
- What felt easy: [TOPICS THAT CLICKED]
- What still feels hard: [TOPICS YOU STRUGGLE WITH]
- My goal: [WHAT I WANT TO BE ABLE TO DO — e.g., "get
  a job," "freelance," "use it for my own projects,"
  "become advanced"]

Output:
1. My current level assessment (honest, not
   flattering):
   - What I can do well
   - What needs more work
   - Blind spots I might not realize

2. Gap-filling plan:
   - The 2-3 specific areas to strengthen
   - One exercise for each area
   - Resources to review

3. Next-level roadmap:
   - What to learn next to go from capable to skilled
   - 3 intermediate/advanced topics to tackle
   - A more complex project idea for the next level

4. How to keep improving:
   - Daily/weekly habits that build this skill over time
   - Communities or groups to join
   - How to get feedback on my work
```

## Pro Tips

- **Don't skip practice to learn more theory.** The biggest mistake self-learners make is consuming content without doing anything. Step 3 exists for a reason.
- **Build something real as soon as possible.** You'll learn more from one project than from 10 tutorials.
- **Embrace being bad at first.** Every expert was terrible when they started. The goal isn't perfection — it's progress.
- **Learn in public.** Share what you're learning on social media or a blog. Teaching others is the fastest way to solidify your own understanding.
- **When stuck, describe your problem clearly.** The better you describe what you're struggling with, the better help you'll get. Say what you tried, what happened, and what you expected.

## Common Mistakes

| Mistake | Why It Hurts You | Do This Instead |
|---------|-----------------|-----------------|
| Watching tutorials without practicing | You feel like you're learning, but you're not | Do the exercises in Step 3 after every lesson |
| Trying to learn everything | You spread too thin and master nothing | Follow the roadmap in order, one stage at a time |
| Skipping the project | You can't prove your skills | Build something real in Step 4, even if it's small |
| Comparing yourself to experts | You get discouraged and quit | Compare yourself to where YOU were last week |
| Not asking for help when stuck | You waste hours on something a 2-minute explanation could fix | Ask the AI, ask communities, ask people |

## Quick Start

Want to start learning right now?

1. Do **Step 1** — get your roadmap (10 minutes)
2. Do **Step 2** — learn the first topic (20 minutes)
3. Do **Step 3** — practice it (15 minutes)
4. Repeat Steps 2-3 for each topic in Stage 1
5. Do **Step 4** when you've finished the first 2 stages
