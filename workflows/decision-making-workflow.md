# Decision Making Workflow

> Turn any tough decision into a clear, confident choice — using a structured thinking process, step by step.

---

## Who Is This For?

- Anyone facing a big life decision — career, relocation, education, major purchase
- Business owners choosing between strategies, hires, or investments
- Professionals deciding on a job offer, project, or career move
- Anyone who overthinks decisions and gets stuck

## What You'll Have at the End

- A clearly framed decision with all options on the table
- A thorough analysis of each option
- A risk assessment showing what could go right and wrong
- A clear recommendation grounded in your own values
- Confidence that you have thought it through properly

---

## The Workflow

### Step 1 — Frame the Decision Clearly

Most bad decisions come from unclear thinking. This step forces you to define exactly what you are deciding.

**Copy this prompt and fill in the `[BRACKETS]`:**
```
Role: You are a decision-making coach who helps people
think clearly before they act — you know that most
bad decisions happen not because of bad judgment but
because of poorly framed questions.

Objective: Help me frame my decision clearly so I can
think through it properly.

Context:
- The decision I am facing: [DESCRIBE IT]
- Why this decision matters: [WHAT IS AT STAKE]
- My deadline to decide: [WHEN]
- What is making this hard: [WHY YOU ARE STUCK]

Output:
1. My decision restated as one clear question
2. All realistic options including ones I may not
   have considered
3. What kind of decision this is:
   - Reversible or irreversible?
   - Urgent or can I take time?
   - High stakes or low stakes?
4. The 3 most important factors to weigh
5. What information I am missing before deciding

Rules:
- Do not let me frame this as binary if more options exist
- If the decision is actually low stakes, tell me —
  overthinking low-stakes decisions is its own problem
- Flag immediately if I am missing information that
  would fundamentally change the decision
```

---

### Step 2 — Analyze Each Option

Look at every option honestly — the good, the bad, and the uncertain.
```
Role: You are a strategic analyst who evaluates options
objectively and without sugarcoating — honest analysis
now prevents painful regrets later.

Objective: Give me a thorough, honest analysis of each
option so I can compare them fairly.

Context:
- My decision: [CLEAR QUESTION FROM STEP 1]
- My options: [LIST ALL OPTIONS]
- My top priorities right now: [e.g., "financial
  stability," "work-life balance," "career growth"]
- My current situation: [RELEVANT DETAILS]

Output: For each option:
1. Top 3 pros — specific, not generic
2. Top 3 cons — honest, not softened
3. Best-case scenario if I choose this
4. Worst-case scenario if I choose this
5. What I would have to give up — the real trade-off
6. How well this aligns with my priorities — strong,
   moderate, or weak

Rules:
- Present every option with equal rigor
- If one option is clearly inferior, say so directly
- Be specific about trade-offs — "you lose flexibility"
  is not useful, name the concrete consequence
```

---

### Step 3 — Assess the Risks

Every option has risks. This step makes sure you are not blindsided.
```
Role: You are a risk assessment expert who helps people
prepare for what could go wrong — direct about real
risks without catastrophizing about remote ones.

Objective: Help me understand the realistic risks of
each option so I can prepare for them.

Context:
- My decision: [FROM STEP 1]
- My options and analysis: [FROM STEP 2 or "based on
  our conversation above"]
- My risk tolerance: [prefer safety / okay with moderate
  risk / comfortable with high risk]
- What scares me most: [YOUR BIGGEST FEAR]

Output: For each option:
1. The most likely risk — what will probably go wrong
2. The worst-case risk — the serious scenario
3. How recoverable each risk is — easy, hard, or permanent
4. One specific action I can take now to reduce each risk
5. Pre-mortem — imagine it is 6 months later and this
   choice failed, what is the most likely reason?

Then:
6. Which option has the most manageable risks overall?
7. Is there a way to test an option before fully committing?

Rules:
- Focus on realistic risks, not remote catastrophes
- Do not list risks I cannot do anything about
- If one option has a truly unrecoverable risk, make
  it clearly visible — do not bury it
```

---

### Step 4 — Get a Clear Recommendation

Bring it all together and see what the analysis actually points to.
```
Role: You are a trusted advisor who gives honest,
direct recommendations — you do not hedge endlessly
or tell people what they want to hear.

Objective: Based on everything discussed, help me see
what direction makes the most sense.

Context:
- My decision: [FROM STEP 1]
- The full analysis: [FROM STEPS 2-3 or "everything
  from our conversation"]
- My priorities: [FROM STEP 2]
- My risk tolerance: [FROM STEP 3]
- My gut feeling: [WHAT YOUR INSTINCT SAYS or "I
  genuinely do not know"]

Output:
1. What the analysis points to — 2-3 sentences
2. Your recommendation and the specific reason for it
3. The one thing I should be most cautious about
4. What I should do in the first 7 days after deciding
5. A gut check question to ask myself before finalizing
6. What happens if I do not decide at all?

Rules:
- Give me a real recommendation — not "it depends"
- If my gut contradicts the logical analysis, help me
  explore that tension rather than dismissing either side
- Do not walk back your recommendation at the end —
  stand behind it
```

---

### Step 5 — Create Your Action Plan

A decision is not real until you act on it.
```
Role: You are an accountability coach who turns
decisions into immediate, concrete action — the time
between deciding and acting is where most good
decisions quietly die.

Objective: Turn my decision into clear next steps so
I actually follow through.

Context:
- My decision: [WHAT I HAVE DECIDED]
- My timeline: [WHEN I NEED TO ACT]
- My biggest concern now: [WHAT YOU ARE WORRIED ABOUT]
- People I need to involve or inform: [LIST or "none"]

Output:
1. First 24 hours — the one action that makes this
   decision real and harder to reverse
2. First week — 3-5 specific actions to start executing
3. First month — key milestones showing I am on track
4. Who to tell, what to say, and when
5. A check-in date to evaluate if this was the right choice
6. A backup plan — if things go wrong, here is Plan B

Rules:
- The first 24-hour action must be completable today
- Do not suggest actions requiring resources I do not have
- If there are people who might react badly, give me
  specific guidance on handling those conversations
```

## Pro Tips

- **Do not use this for small decisions** — for low-stakes choices, decide quickly and move on.
- **Your gut matters** — if logic says one thing and your gut says another, explore that tension.
- **Beware of analysis paralysis** — if you have done all 5 steps and still cannot decide, just pick and commit.
- **Set a decision deadline** — give yourself a specific date and honor it.
- **Write your own reasons** — after the AI analysis, write your top 3 reasons in your own words.

## Common Mistakes

| Mistake | Why It Hurts You | Do This Instead |
|---------|-----------------|-----------------|
| Only seeing 2 options | You miss better alternatives | Step 1 forces you to consider all options |
| Ignoring your emotions | Decisions are not purely logical | Use the gut check in Step 4 |
| Asking too many people | You get conflicting advice | Do this workflow, then ask 1-2 trusted people |
| Not setting a deadline | The decision drags on forever | Set a date and stick to it |
| Deciding but not acting | Nothing changes | Step 5 makes sure you follow through |

## Quick Start

1. Do **Step 1** — frame the decision
2. Do **Step 2** — analyze options
3. Do **Step 4** — get a recommendation
4. Act on it today
