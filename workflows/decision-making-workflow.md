# Decision Making Workflow

> Turn any tough decision into a clear, confident choice — using a structured thinking process, step by step.

---

## Who Is This For?

- Anyone facing a big life decision (career, relocation, relationship, education)
- Business owners choosing between strategies, hires, or investments
- Professionals deciding on a job offer, project, or career move
- Anyone who overthinks decisions and gets stuck

## What You'll Have at the End

- A clearly framed decision with all options laid out
- A thorough pros and cons breakdown for each option
- A risk assessment showing what could go right and wrong
- A clear recommendation backed by your own values and priorities
- Confidence that you've thought it through properly

---

## The Workflow

### Step 1 — Frame the Decision Clearly

Most bad decisions come from unclear thinking. This step forces you to define exactly what you're deciding and why it matters.

**Copy this prompt and fill in the `[BRACKETS]`:**

```
Role: You are a decision-making coach who helps people
think clearly about important choices.

Objective: Help me frame my decision clearly so I can
think through it properly.

Context:
- The decision I'm facing: [DESCRIBE IT — e.g., "Should
  I take this new job or stay at my current company?"
  or "Should I launch product A or product B first?"]
- Why this decision matters: [WHAT'S AT STAKE]
- My deadline to decide: [WHEN YOU NEED TO DECIDE]
- What's making this hard: [WHY YOU'RE STUCK — e.g.,
  "both options seem good," "I'm scared of the risk,"
  "too many unknowns"]

Output: Help me by:
1. Restating my decision as one clear question
2. Listing ALL my realistic options (I might be missing
   some — include options I haven't considered)
3. Identifying what kind of decision this is:
   - Reversible or irreversible?
   - Urgent or can I take time?
   - High stakes or low stakes?
4. The 3 most important factors I should weigh
5. What information I might be missing before deciding
```

---

### Step 2 — Analyze Each Option

Now look at every option honestly — the good, the bad, and the uncertain.

```
Role: You are a strategic analyst who evaluates options
objectively, without sugarcoating.

Objective: Give me a thorough, honest analysis of each
option so I can compare them fairly.

Context:
- My decision: [CLEAR QUESTION FROM STEP 1]
- My options: [LIST ALL OPTIONS FROM STEP 1]
- My top priorities in life/work right now: [e.g.,
  "financial stability," "work-life balance," "career
  growth," "freedom," "family time"]
- My current situation: [RELEVANT DETAILS — e.g.,
  current salary, job satisfaction, savings, family
  situation]

Output: For EACH option, give me:
1. Top 3 Pros (specific, not generic)
2. Top 3 Cons (be honest, don't soften them)
3. Best-case scenario if I choose this
4. Worst-case scenario if I choose this
5. What I'd have to give up (the real tradeoff)
6. How this aligns with my priorities (rate: strong
   / moderate / weak)

Present this as a clear comparison so I can see all
options side by side.
```

---

### Step 3 — Assess the Risks

Every option has risks. This step makes sure you're not blindsided.

```
Role: You are a risk assessment expert who helps people
prepare for what could go wrong — without being overly
negative.

Objective: Help me understand the risks of each option
so I can prepare for them.

Context:
- My decision: [FROM STEP 1]
- My options and their pros/cons: [FROM STEP 2 or "based
  on our conversation above"]
- My risk tolerance: [I prefer safety / I'm okay with
  moderate risk / I'm comfortable with high risk]
- What scares me most about this decision: [YOUR BIGGEST
  FEAR]

Output: For each option:
1. The most likely risk (what will probably go wrong)
2. The worst-case risk (the nightmare scenario)
3. How recoverable each risk is (easy to fix / hard to
   fix / permanent)
4. What I can do RIGHT NOW to reduce each risk
5. A "pre-mortem" — imagine it's 6 months later and
   this choice failed. What's the most likely reason
   it failed?

Then tell me:
6. Which option has the most manageable risks overall?
7. Is there a way to test or try an option before fully
   committing?
```

---

### Step 4 — Get a Clear Recommendation

Now bring it all together. The AI won't make the decision for you, but it can help you see what the analysis points to.

```
Role: You are a trusted advisor who gives honest,
thoughtful recommendations — not just what people
want to hear.

Objective: Based on everything we've discussed, help
me see what direction makes the most sense.

Context:
- My decision: [FROM STEP 1]
- The full analysis: [FROM STEPS 2-3 or "everything
  from our conversation"]
- My priorities: [FROM STEP 2]
- My risk tolerance: [FROM STEP 3]
- My gut feeling (if I have one): [WHAT YOUR INSTINCT
  SAYS or "I genuinely don't know"]

Output:
1. Summary of what the analysis points to (2-3
   sentences)
2. Your recommendation and why (be specific and direct)
3. The ONE thing I should be cautious about with this
   choice
4. What I should do in the first 7 days after deciding
5. A "gut check" question I should ask myself before
   making it final:
   - "If I imagine living with Option A for a year,
     do I feel relief or regret?"
6. A reminder: what happens if I don't decide at all?
   (sometimes NOT deciding is the worst option)
```

**After getting the recommendation, ask yourself:**

- Does this recommendation feel right or does something feel off?
- If it feels off, tell the AI: *"Your recommendation is X, but something feels wrong. Help me figure out what's bothering me."*

---

### Step 5 — Create Your Action Plan

A decision isn't real until you act on it. This prevents "decision limbo."

```
Role: You are an accountability coach who helps people
turn decisions into immediate action.

Objective: Turn my decision into concrete next steps
so I actually follow through.

Context:
- My decision: [WHAT I'VE DECIDED]
- When I need to act: [TIMELINE]
- What I'm most worried about now that I've decided:
  [YOUR CONCERN]
- People I need to tell or involve: [LIST THEM or "none"]

Output:
1. First 24 hours: The ONE thing I should do right now
   to make this decision real and irreversible
2. First week: 3-5 specific actions to start executing
3. First month: Key milestones that show I'm on track
4. People to tell: Who to inform, what to say, and when
5. A "check-in" plan: When should I evaluate if this
   was the right choice? (suggest a specific date)
6. A backup plan: If things go wrong, here's my
   Plan B (2-3 sentences)
```

## Pro Tips

- **Don't use this for small decisions.** This workflow is for decisions that genuinely affect your life or business. For small stuff, just flip a coin and move on.
- **Your gut matters.** If the logical analysis says one thing but your gut says another, explore that tension. Ask the AI to help you figure out why.
- **Beware of "analysis paralysis."** If you've done all 5 steps and still can't decide, the options are probably close enough that either one will work. Just pick and commit.
- **Write your own reasons.** After the AI gives its analysis, write down YOUR top 3 reasons in your own words. If you can't, you might not be ready to decide.
- **Set a decision deadline.** The worst outcome is often no decision at all. Give yourself a specific date and commit to choosing by then.

## Common Mistakes

| Mistake | Why It Hurts You | Do This Instead |
|---------|-----------------|-----------------|
| Only seeing 2 options | You miss better alternatives | Step 1 forces you to consider all options |
| Ignoring your emotions | Decisions aren't purely logical | Use the gut check in Step 4 |
| Asking too many people | You get conflicting advice and more confusion | Do this workflow, then ask 1-2 trusted people to confirm |
| Not setting a deadline | The decision drags on forever | Set a date in Step 1 and stick to it |
| Deciding but not acting | Nothing changes | Step 5 makes sure you follow through |

---

## Quick Start

Need to decide fast? Minimum path:

1. Do **Step 1** — frame the decision (5 minutes)
2. Do **Step 2** — analyze options (10 minutes)
3. Do **Step 4** — get a recommendation (5 minutes)
4. Act on it today
