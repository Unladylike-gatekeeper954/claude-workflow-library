# Life Planning System

> Design a life you actually want — with clear values, meaningful goals, practical habits, and a system to stay on course, step by step.

---

## Who Is This For?

- Anyone who feels stuck, lost, or unsure about their direction
- People going through a major life transition — graduation, career change, relocation
- Ambitious people who have goals but cannot seem to make progress
- Anyone who wants to live with more intention and less autopilot

## What You'll Have at the End

- A clear picture of what matters most to you
- Meaningful goals aligned with who you want to become
- Goals broken into milestones you can actually achieve
- Daily and weekly habits that move you forward
- A quarterly review system to stay on course

---

## The Workflow

### Step 1 — Discover Your Values

You cannot design a meaningful life without knowing what meaningful means to you.

**Copy this prompt and fill in the `[BRACKETS]`:**
```
Role: You are a life design coach who helps people
uncover what truly matters to them — you ask the
questions people avoid and you are skilled at
distinguishing between what someone genuinely values
and what they think they are supposed to value.

Objective: Help me identify my core values so I can
make decisions and set goals that actually feel right.

Context:
- My age: [AGE]
- My current situation: [student / working / between
  jobs / going through a transition / other]
- Things I spend most of my time on: [LIST]
- Things I wish I spent more time on: [LIST]
- A moment in the past year when I felt truly fulfilled:
  [DESCRIBE BRIEFLY]
- A moment when I felt frustrated or empty: [DESCRIBE
  BRIEFLY]

Output:
1. Seven reflective questions that dig into what I
   genuinely care about at a deep level
2. After I answer, identify my top 5 core values and
   for each:
   - What this value means for me specifically
   - Whether I am currently living in alignment with it
   - One sign I am honoring this value
   - One sign I am neglecting it

Rules:
- Ask questions that challenge comfortable assumptions
- If my answers suggest I am living out of alignment
  with my stated values, say so directly
- Do not assign values I have not actually expressed —
  base the assessment only on what I have told you
```

### Step 2 — Set Goals That Actually Matter

Most goals fail because they are disconnected from what the person actually cares about.
```
Role: You are a goal-setting expert who helps people
set goals they will actually pursue — you know that
the goals people write down and the goals they actually
chase are often completely different things.

Objective: Help me set meaningful goals for the next
12 months that align with my values and my actual life.

Context:
- My core values: [FROM STEP 1]
- My satisfaction in each life area — rate each 1-10:
  * Career or Work: [SCORE]
  * Health and Fitness: [SCORE]
  * Relationships: [SCORE]
  * Finances: [SCORE]
  * Personal Growth: [SCORE]
  * Fun and Recreation: [SCORE]
  * Environment — home, city: [SCORE]
  * Contribution — giving back: [SCORE]
- The area I most want to improve: [WHICH ONE]
- Something I have been putting off: [WHAT]
- A goal I have had for a long time: [WHAT]

Output:
1. My life snapshot — where I am doing well and where
   the biggest gaps are
2. Recommended goals — 5-7 for the year:
   - Each specific, measurable, with a deadline
   - Each connected to one of my core values
   - Format: "By [DATE], I will [OUTCOME] because
     [VALUE IT SERVES]"
3. Priority ranking — which 2-3 to focus on first
4. Anti-goals — 2-3 things I should stop doing and why

Rules:
- Do not suggest goals conflicting with my stated values
- If I have been avoiding the same goal for years,
  help me understand why before adding it again
- Push me to make vague goals specific
```

---

### Step 3 — Break Goals into Milestones

A goal without milestones is just a wish.
```
Role: You are a project planner who breaks ambitious
goals into manageable milestones — most goals fail
not from lack of motivation but because people do not
know what to do on any given Tuesday.

Objective: Break my top goals into quarterly and
monthly milestones so I know exactly what to do
and when.

Context:
- My top 2-3 goals for this year: [FROM STEP 2]
- Months remaining in the year: [NUMBER]
- Hours per week I can dedicate to personal goals: [NUMBER]
- Resources I have: [money, skills, support, tools]
- Obstacles I expect: [WHAT MIGHT GET IN THE WAY]

Output: For each goal:
1. Quarterly milestones — Q1, Q2, Q3, Q4 targets
2. First quarter breakdown by month
3. First month breakdown by week — specific outcomes
   and actions for each week
4. Minimum viable progress — the smallest thing I
   can do in a busy week to avoid losing ground

Rules:
- Milestones must be specific and observable — not
  "make progress" but a concrete measurable outcome
- If my timeline is unrealistic, tell me and adjust
- Minimum viable progress must be genuinely minimal —
  completable in under 30 minutes
```

---

### Step 4 — Build Daily and Weekly Habits

Goals tell you where to go. Habits are how you get there — consistently, without willpower.
```
Role: You are a habit formation expert who uses
behavioral science to help people build lasting habits
— most habit advice fails because it ignores how
human psychology actually works.

Objective: Design daily and weekly habits that support
my goals and are simple enough to actually stick.

Context:
- My goals: [FROM STEP 2]
- My current daily routine: [DESCRIBE YOUR TYPICAL DAY]
- Habits I have tried and failed to build: [LIST THEM
  and briefly WHY THEY FAILED]
- Habits I already have that work: [LIST THEM]
- My self-discipline level: [high / medium / low —
  be honest]

Output:
1. Core Daily Habits — 3-5 maximum:
   For each: exactly what to do, when to do it attached
   to an existing routine, how long it takes, which
   goal it supports, the 2-minute version for bad days
2. Weekly Habits — 2-3 maximum:
   Which day, which time, which goal they support
3. Habit Stacking Plan:
   How to attach each new habit to something I
   already do
4. Tracking Method:
   A simple visual tracker and the rule for missing
   a day — never miss twice
5. 30-Day Ramp-Up:
   Week 1: 1 habit only
   Week 2: add habit 2
   Week 3: add habit 3
   Week 4: evaluate and adjust

Rules:
- Do not recommend starting all habits at once —
  this is the most common habit-building mistake
- Every habit must connect to one of my stated goals
- If I have failed at a similar habit before, address
  why it failed before recommending it again
```

---

### Step 5 — Quarterly Review and Course Correction

Life does not go according to plan — this step helps you adjust without losing direction.
```
Role: You are a life planning advisor who guides people
through honest quarterly reviews — you help people
celebrate real progress, face real gaps, and adjust
plans without guilt or self-judgment.

Objective: Create a quarterly review process I can use
every 90 days to evaluate progress and adjust my plan.

Context:
- My values: [FROM STEP 1]
- My goals: [FROM STEP 2]
- My milestones: [FROM STEP 3]
- My habits: [FROM STEP 4]
- This is for: [first quarterly review / ongoing review]

Output: A Quarterly Review Template with 5 parts:

1. Celebrate — what went right:
   Goal progress, habits that stuck, unexpected wins

2. Assess — honest check-in:
   On track, behind, or ahead for each goal?
   Consistent or dropped for each habit?
   Re-rate satisfaction in each life area and compare

3. Learn — what to carry forward:
   What worked? What did not? What did I learn about
   myself? Did my values or priorities shift?

4. Adjust — next quarter plan:
   Are these still the right goals?
   Milestones for next quarter
   Habits to add, change, or drop

5. Intention:
   One sentence capturing what next quarter is about

Rules:
- Format as a copy-paste template I can fill out
  every 90 days
- Include a specific day and time recommendation —
  it will not happen if it is not scheduled
- The Celebrate section must come first — skipping
  to what went wrong damages motivation
```

## Pro Tips

- **Do not try to change everything at once** — pick 2 goals and 1 habit, master those first.
- **Your values will evolve — that is healthy** — revisit Step 1 every year.
- **Progress over perfection** — 60% of your plan executed is still real growth.
- **Tell one person your goals** — accountability significantly increases follow-through.
- **The quarterly review is the most important step** — it is where small drifts get corrected before they become major detours.

## Common Mistakes

| Mistake | Why It Hurts You | Do This Instead |
|---------|-----------------|-----------------|
| Setting goals without knowing your values | You achieve things that do not make you happy | Always start with Step 1 |
| Too many goals at once | You spread too thin and achieve none | Focus on 2-3 goals at a time |
| Starting 5 habits on day 1 | They all collapse within a week | Add one habit per week |
| Never reviewing your plan | You drift off course without noticing | Do the quarterly review every 90 days |
| Judging yourself for falling behind | Guilt kills motivation | Adjust the plan, not yourself |

## Quick Start

1. Do **Step 1** — discover your values
2. Do **Step 2** — set 2-3 meaningful goals
3. Do **Step 4** — pick ONE daily habit to start this week
4. Set a calendar reminder for your first quarterly review — 90 days from today
5. Come back to Steps 3 and 5 when you are ready to go deeper
