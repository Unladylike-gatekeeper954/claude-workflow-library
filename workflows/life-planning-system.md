# 🧭 Life Planning System

> Design a life you actually want — with clear values, meaningful goals, practical habits, and a system to stay on course, step by step.

---

## Who Is This For?

- Anyone who feels stuck, lost, or unsure about their direction
- People going through a major life transition (graduation, career change, breakup, relocation)
- Ambitious people who have goals but can't seem to make progress
- Anyone who wants to live with more intention and less autopilot

## What You'll Have at the End

- A clear picture of what matters most to you (your values)
- Meaningful goals aligned with who you want to become
- Goals broken into milestones you can actually achieve
- Daily and weekly habits that move you forward
- A quarterly review system to stay on course

---

## The Workflow

### Step 1 — Discover Your Values

You can't plan a meaningful life without knowing what "meaningful" means to you. This step helps you figure that out.

**Copy this prompt and fill in the `[BRACKETS]`:**

```
Role: You are a life design coach who helps people
uncover what truly matters to them — beyond what
society, family, or social media says should matter.

Objective: Help me identify my core values so I can
make decisions and set goals that actually feel right.

Context:
- My age: [AGE]
- My current life situation: [student / working /
  between jobs / starting a family / going through a
  transition / other]
- Things I spend most of my time on: [LIST]
- Things I wish I spent more time on: [LIST]
- A moment in the past year when I felt truly happy
  or fulfilled: [DESCRIBE IT BRIEFLY]
- A moment when I felt frustrated or empty: [DESCRIBE
  IT BRIEFLY]

Output:
1. Ask me 7 reflective questions — one at a time would
   be best, but list them all so I can think through
   them. These should dig into what I care about at a
   deep level (not surface-level stuff like "do you
   like travel?").

2. After I answer (or based on what I've shared),
   identify my top 5 core values from this list (or
   suggest ones not on this list):
   - Freedom, Security, Family, Adventure, Growth,
     Creativity, Health, Wealth, Impact, Connection,
     Independence, Knowledge, Peace, Status, Faith,
     Fun, Justice, Legacy, Simplicity, Leadership

3. For each value:
   - What this value means for ME specifically (not
     a dictionary definition)
   - Am I currently living in alignment with it?
     (honest assessment)
   - One sign that I'm honoring this value in daily
     life
   - One sign that I'm neglecting it
```

**Take your time with this step.** Your values are the foundation for everything else.

### Step 2 — Set Goals That Actually Matter

Most goals fail because they're disconnected from what the person actually cares about. This step connects your goals to your values.

```
Role: You are a goal-setting expert who helps people
set goals they'll actually pursue — not just goals
that sound impressive.

Objective: Help me set meaningful goals for the next
12 months that align with my values and current life.

Context:
- My core values: [YOUR TOP 5 FROM STEP 1]
- My current life areas and satisfaction level (rate
  each 1-10):
  * Career/Work: [SCORE]
  * Health/Fitness: [SCORE]
  * Relationships: [SCORE]
  * Finances: [SCORE]
  * Personal Growth: [SCORE]
  * Fun/Recreation: [SCORE]
  * Environment (home, city): [SCORE]
  * Contribution (giving back): [SCORE]
- The area I most want to improve: [WHICH ONE]
- Something I've been putting off: [WHAT]
- A dream I've had for a while: [WHAT]

Output:
1. My Life Snapshot:
   - Where I'm doing well (celebrate this)
   - Where the biggest gaps are (focus here)
   - The connection between my gaps and my values

2. Recommended Goals (5-7 goals for the year):
   - Each goal is specific, measurable, and has a
     deadline
   - Each goal connects to one of my core values
     (show the connection)
   - Each goal is challenging but realistic given
     my situation
   - Format: "By [DATE], I will [SPECIFIC OUTCOME]
     because [VALUE IT SERVES]"

3. Priority Ranking:
   - Which 2-3 goals should I focus on FIRST?
   - Why these ones first? (logical sequencing)

4. The Anti-Goals:
   - 2-3 things I should STOP doing this year
   - Why each one is holding me back
```

### Step 3 — Break Goals into Milestones

A goal without milestones is just a wish. This step turns each goal into a step-by-step path.

```
Role: You are a project planner who breaks big goals
into manageable, week-by-week milestones.

Objective: Break my top goals into quarterly and
monthly milestones so I know exactly what to do and
when.

Context:
- My top 2-3 goals for this year: [FROM STEP 2]
- Months remaining in the year: [NUMBER]
- Hours per week I can dedicate to personal goals:
  [NUMBER]
- Resources I have: [money, skills, support network,
  tools — be specific]
- Obstacles I expect: [WHAT MIGHT GET IN THE WAY]

Output: For each goal:

1. Quarterly Milestones:
   - Q1: Where I should be by end of quarter 1
   - Q2: Where I should be by end of quarter 2
   - Q3: Where I should be by end of quarter 3
   - Q4: Final target

2. First Quarter Breakdown (monthly):
   - Month 1: Specific outcomes and actions
   - Month 2: Specific outcomes and actions
   - Month 3: Specific outcomes and actions

3. First Month Breakdown (weekly):
   - Week 1: What to do and what "done" looks like
   - Week 2: What to do and what "done" looks like
   - Week 3: What to do and what "done" looks like
   - Week 4: What to do and what "done" looks like

4. Minimum Viable Progress:
   - What's the SMALLEST thing I can do this week
     to start making progress on this goal?
   - (This is my safety net for busy weeks)
```

### Step 4 — Build Daily and Weekly Habits

Goals tell you WHERE to go. Habits are HOW you get there — on autopilot.

```
Role: You are a habit formation expert who helps people
build lasting habits using proven behavioral science.

Objective: Design daily and weekly habits that support
my goals — habits that are simple enough to actually
stick.

Context:
- My goals: [FROM STEP 2]
- My current daily routine: [DESCRIBE YOUR TYPICAL DAY
  — wake time, work, meals, free time, bedtime]
- Habits I've tried and failed to build: [LIST THEM
  and WHY THEY FAILED]
- Habits I already have that work: [LIST THEM]
- My willpower level: [high — I'm disciplined / medium
  — I'm inconsistent / low — I struggle with follow-
  through]

Output:
1. Core Daily Habits (3-5 max):
   - For each habit:
     * What to do (be extremely specific)
     * When to do it (tie it to an existing habit or
       time)
     * How long it takes (keep it under 15 minutes
       to start)
     * Which goal it supports
     * The "minimum version" for bad days (2-minute
       version)

2. Weekly Habits (2-3 max):
   - Habits that happen once a week
   - Which day and time to do them
   - How they support my goals

3. Habit Stacking Plan:
   - How to attach new habits to things I already do
   - Example: "After I [EXISTING HABIT], I will
     [NEW HABIT]"

4. Tracking Method:
   - A simple visual tracker (don't overcomplicate)
   - The "don't break the chain" approach
   - What to do when I miss a day (the rule: never
     miss twice)

5. The 30-Day Ramp-Up:
   - Week 1: Start with just 1 habit (the easiest one)
   - Week 2: Add habit 2
   - Week 3: Add habit 3
   - Week 4: Evaluate and adjust
   - Why I should NOT start all habits on day 1
```

### Step 5 — Quarterly Review and Course Correction

Life doesn't go according to plan — and that's okay. This step helps you adjust without losing direction.

```
Role: You are a life planning advisor who helps people
reflect honestly and adjust their plans without guilt.

Objective: Create a quarterly review process I can use
to evaluate my progress, celebrate wins, and adjust
my plan.

Context:
- My values: [FROM STEP 1]
- My goals: [FROM STEP 2]
- My milestones: [FROM STEP 3]
- My habits: [FROM STEP 4]
- This is for: [my first quarterly review / ongoing
  reviews]

Output: Create a Quarterly Review Template with:

1. CELEBRATE (what went right):
   - What goals did I make progress on?
   - What habits stuck?
   - What am I most proud of?
   - What unexpected good things happened?

2. ASSESS (honest check-in):
   - For each goal: Am I on track, behind, or ahead?
   - For each habit: Am I consistent, inconsistent,
     or did I drop it?
   - Rate my satisfaction in each life area again
     (1-10) and compare to last quarter
   - What surprised me this quarter?

3. LEARN (what to take forward):
   - What worked well that I should keep doing?
   - What didn't work that I should change or drop?
   - What did I learn about myself?
   - Did my values or priorities shift? (this is okay
     and normal)

4. ADJUST (next quarter plan):
   - Are my goals still the right goals? (it's okay
     to change them)
   - What milestones should I set for next quarter?
   - Do I need to add, remove, or change any habits?
   - What's my #1 focus for the next 90 days?

5. INTENTION:
   - Write one sentence that captures what the next
     quarter is about for me.
   - Example: "This quarter is about building the
     foundation for my career change."

Give me this as a copy-paste template I can fill out
every 3 months.
```

## Pro Tips

- **Don't try to change everything at once.** Pick your top 2-3 goals and 1-2 habits. Master those before adding more.
- **Your values will change — and that's good.** Revisit Step 1 every year. What mattered at 22 might not matter at 30.
- **Progress over perfection.** A month where you did 60% of your plan is still a month of growth. Most people do 0%.
- **Tell someone your goals.** Accountability dramatically increases follow-through. Share your goals with one trusted person.
- **The quarterly review is the most important step.** Life will throw curveballs. The review is where you adjust your plan without abandoning it.

## Common Mistakes

| Mistake | Why It Hurts You | Do This Instead |
|---------|-----------------|-----------------|
| Setting goals without knowing your values | You achieve things that don't make you happy | Always start with Step 1 |
| Too many goals at once | You spread too thin and achieve none | Focus on 2-3 goals at a time |
| Starting 5 habits on day 1 | They all collapse within a week | Add one habit per week (Step 4) |
| Never reviewing your plan | You drift off course and don't notice | Do the quarterly review every 90 days (Step 5) |
| Beating yourself up for falling behind | Guilt kills motivation | Adjust the plan, don't judge yourself |

## Quick Start

Start living with more intention today:

1. Do **Step 1** — discover your values (15 minutes of reflection)
2. Do **Step 2** — set 2-3 meaningful goals (15 minutes)
3. Do **Step 4** — pick ONE daily habit to start this week
4. Set a calendar reminder for your first quarterly review (90 days from now)
5. Come back to Steps 3 and 5 when you're ready to go deeper
