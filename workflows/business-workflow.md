# Business Workflow

> Go from a rough business idea to a clear plan with market research, a business model, and actionable next steps — step by step.

---

## Who Is This For?

- First-time entrepreneurs exploring a business idea
- Freelancers looking to turn their skills into a business
- Small business owners rethinking their strategy
- Anyone who wants to validate an idea before investing time or money

## What You'll Have at the End

- A clear, refined business idea
- Market research showing who your customers are and what competitors exist
- A simple business model (how you'll make money)
- Basic financial projections
- A 30-day action plan to get started

---

## The Workflow

### Step 1 — Clarify and Sharpen Your Idea

Most business ideas are too vague. This step forces you to get specific about what you're building and who it's for.

**Copy this prompt and fill in the `[BRACKETS]`:**

```
Role: You are a startup advisor who has helped hundreds
of founders refine their business ideas.

Objective: Help me turn my rough business idea into a
clear, specific concept.

Context:
- My idea: [DESCRIBE YOUR IDEA — even if it's messy or
  incomplete, that's fine]
- Problem I'm trying to solve: [WHAT PROBLEM or "I'm
  not sure yet"]
- Who I think my customer is: [WHO or "I'm not sure yet"]
- My skills/background: [WHAT YOU'RE GOOD AT]
- My budget to start: [low (under $500) / medium
  ($500-$5000) / high ($5000+)]

Output: Give me:
1. My idea rewritten in one clear sentence (the kind
   you'd say at a dinner party)
2. The specific problem this solves and who has that
   problem
3. What makes this different from what already exists
4. 3 things that could make this idea succeed
5. 3 things that could make this idea fail
6. One question I should answer before going further
```

---

### Step 2 — Research Your Market

You need to know who your customers are, how big the opportunity is, and who you're competing with.

```
Role: You are a market research analyst who explains
findings in plain, actionable language.

Objective: Give me a clear picture of the market for
my business idea.

Context:
- My business idea: [ONE-SENTENCE VERSION FROM STEP 1]
- My target customer: [FROM STEP 1]
- Industry/category: [e.g., fitness, SaaS, food,
  education, e-commerce]
- Location: [local / national / global / online only]

Output: Give me:
1. Target Customer Profile:
   - Who they are (age, job, lifestyle)
   - What they currently do to solve this problem
   - How much they typically spend on solutions like this
   - Where they hang out (online and offline)

2. Market Size:
   - Is this a growing, stable, or shrinking market?
   - Roughly how many potential customers exist?

3. Competitor Analysis:
   - Name 3-5 existing competitors (or similar solutions)
   - What each does well
   - What each does poorly (your opportunity)
   - Their approximate pricing

4. Key Insight:
   - The one gap in the market that my idea could fill
```

**After you get this, ask:**

```
Based on this market research, what would you say is
the biggest risk with my idea? And what's the biggest
opportunity I should focus on?
```

### Step 3 — Build Your Business Model

This is how you'll actually make money. Keep it simple.

```
Role: You are a business model expert who helps
entrepreneurs build simple, realistic revenue plans.

Objective: Help me create a clear business model that
shows how my business will work and make money.

Context:
- My business idea: [ONE-SENTENCE VERSION]
- My target customer: [FROM STEP 2]
- Competitors charge: [PRICING FROM STEP 2]
- My starting budget: [low / medium / high]
- I want to start: [as a side project / full-time /
  I'm not sure yet]

Output: Give me:
1. Revenue Model (how I make money):
   - Pricing structure (what to charge and how —
     one-time, subscription, hourly, etc.)
   - Why this pricing makes sense for my customer
   - Suggested starting price

2. Cost Structure (what I'll spend):
   - One-time startup costs
   - Monthly ongoing costs
   - What I can start without (save money early)

3. Customer Acquisition (how I get customers):
   - 3 ways to find my first 10 customers (free or cheap)
   - 2 ways to scale to 100+ customers
   - Which channels my competitors use

4. Simple Value Chain:
   - What I deliver → How I deliver it → What customer pays
   - Keep it to one clear sentence
```

### Step 4 — Create Basic Financial Projections

You don't need a complex spreadsheet. You need simple math that shows if this can work.

```
Role: You are a financial advisor for small businesses
who keeps things simple and honest.

Objective: Create basic financial projections that show
whether my business can be profitable.

Context:
- My pricing: [FROM STEP 3]
- My monthly costs: [FROM STEP 3]
- My startup costs: [FROM STEP 3]
- How many customers I think I can get in month 1:
  [YOUR ESTIMATE or "give me a realistic estimate"]
- Customer growth rate I'm hoping for: [e.g., "10 new
  customers per month" or "suggest a realistic rate"]

Output: Create a simple 12-month projection:

1. Monthly Revenue & Expense Table:
   | Month | Customers | Revenue | Costs | Profit/Loss |

2. Break-Even Point:
   - How many customers I need to break even
   - How many months that will likely take

3. Best Case vs. Realistic vs. Worst Case:
   - Show all three scenarios in a simple table

4. Key Numbers to Watch:
   - The 3 metrics I should track every week

Keep the math simple. Explain each number so a
non-finance person understands it.
```

---

### Step 5 — Build Your 30-Day Action Plan

Ideas don't fail because they're bad. They fail because people don't take action. This step gives you a clear path forward.

```
Role: You are a business launch coach who helps people
take their first real steps — without overthinking.

Objective: Create a realistic 30-day action plan to
start making progress on my business.

Context:
- My business idea: [ONE-SENTENCE VERSION]
- My current situation: [employed full-time / freelancer /
  student / unemployed / other]
- Hours I can dedicate per week: [NUMBER]
- My budget for the first month: [AMOUNT]
- Skills I have: [LIST THEM]
- Skills I need but don't have: [LIST THEM]
- My biggest fear or blocker: [WHAT'S STOPPING YOU]

Output: Create a 30-day plan:

Week 1 — Validate:
- 3 specific actions to confirm people want this
  (e.g., talk to potential customers, run a survey,
  test a landing page)

Week 2 — Build:
- 3 specific actions to create the simplest version
  of my product/service (MVP)

Week 3 — Launch:
- 3 specific actions to get my first paying customer
  or user

Week 4 — Learn:
- 3 specific actions to gather feedback and decide
  what's next

For each action:
- What exactly to do (step by step)
- How long it should take
- What free tools or resources to use
- What "done" looks like
```

## Pro Tips

- **Talk to real people before building anything.** Ask 5-10 potential customers about the problem you're solving. Their answers will surprise you.
- **Start smaller than you think.** Don't build the full vision on day one. Build the smallest version that someone would pay for.
- **Don't skip the financial projections.** Even rough numbers show you if the math works. Many ideas sound great until you do the math.
- **Revisit this workflow quarterly.** As your business grows, run Steps 2-4 again with updated data.
- **If the AI tells you a risk, take it seriously.** It's easy to brush off concerns about your own idea. The warnings are usually worth hearing.

## Common Mistakes

| Mistake | Why It Hurts You | Do This Instead |
|---------|-----------------|-----------------|
| Falling in love with the idea | You ignore red flags | Let the market tell you if the idea is good |
| Building before validating | You waste time and money | Spend Week 1 on validation, not building |
| Pricing too low | Hard to raise prices later, low margins | Research what competitors charge first |
| Planning forever, never starting | Perfection prevents progress | Follow the 30-day plan — imperfect action beats perfect planning |
| Ignoring competitors | You don't know what you're up against | Do proper competitor research in Step 2 |

## Quick Start

If you're excited and want to move fast:

1. Do **Step 1** to sharpen your idea (10 minutes)
2. Do **Step 2** to check the market (15 minutes)
3. Skip to **Step 5** for your 30-day action plan
4. Come back to Steps 3-4 when you need to plan finances
